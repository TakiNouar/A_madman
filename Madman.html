A Madman's Poem

for the one who made the madman sane

I once stood,
I once loved,
I once tried,
I once died.

From heart to blood,
A man who believed—
The sky would rain gold.
A man who believed—
The days would slow.
A man who believed—
The sun would rise,
No matter how dark the night became.
A man survived...
Until belief became two.
A man died...
When belief was one.

A man's belief
Wasn't enough to lead.
A man's heart
Wasn’t his to give.
A man's heart
Was his to command.

A man stood.
A man loved.
A man tried.
A man died.

Where pens drop,
And hearts clap.
Where men clash,
And swords slash.
Where she walks,
Where she flies.
Where she giggles,
Where she cries.
Where she's there,
Where she's here.
Where she's here,
Where she's not.
Where she's not...
Where she's not...
Where she's not...
Where's she?

A madman when she left.
A madman I was.
A madman I became.
A madman they called me.
And a madman I will remain.
